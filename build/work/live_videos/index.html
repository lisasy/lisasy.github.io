Helping people report live videos showing offensive or flagrant content

Product Design Lead at Facebook (2016)

In early 2016, Facebook shifted its focus to build and ship Live Videos on its platform; I oversaw the product design to conceive, coordinate, design, and execute and shipping its reporting flow so that people can report objectionable, sensitive content that may arise from live video content.

Objective

Empower people to report live videos showing offensive or flagrant content, such as graphic violence, hate speech, nudity and pornography, spam, and more.

What I did

  • Worked with multi-office team to in end-to-end design and development process with colleagues in Menlo Park, Seattle, and London
  • Worked in agile manner in tandem with developing base product of Live
  • Developed product platform and framework to extend potential vertical reporting use cases, ranging from most “minor” (nudity or sex acts) to most critical (suicide and self-injury).
  • Balanced business, policy, and operations needs with user experience to define the end product design.

Results

The Live Video reporting flow was more effective in flagging flagrant content than the existing reporting flow.

  • 1.5x more (i.e. 2.5x NFX counts) policy violating Live broadcasts actioned by CO
  • 7.5x more (i.e. 8.5x NFX counts) distinct people reporting Live broadcasts
  • 5.75x more (i.e. 6.75x NFX counts) distinct Live broadcasts being reviewed by CO
